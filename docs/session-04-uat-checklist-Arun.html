A
Header Keyword Search
Type a word in the top search bar to filter listings by keyword within the current map view
3 checks
⚠ Make sure you are signed in and on the Listings (Browse) tab before starting this block.
1
Search bar at the top shows 🔍 icon and filters the listing panel instantly
  1. Look at the search bar at the very top of the screen — it should show a 🔍 icon and placeholder text "Search listings by keyword…"
  2. Make sure you are on the Listings tab.
  3. Click the search bar and type a word you expect to appear in a listing — e.g. Honda, Sofa, or 2BHK.
  4. The listing panel on the left should immediately narrow to only listings whose title or details contain that word. No page reload happens.
  5. Delete the text — all listings should return.
✅ Expected: Listing panel filters instantly as you type. Deleting the keyword restores all listings. The map pins do not disappear.
2
Keyword search respects the current map view — panning changes results
  1. Type a broad keyword in the search bar (e.g. flat or room).
  2. Look at the listing panel — only listings matching the keyword AND visible within the current map area should appear.
  3. Now pan the map to a different part of Bangalore (drag the map to a new area).
  4. The listing panel should update to show keyword-matching listings from the new map area.
✅ Expected: Results change when you pan the map — only listings that both match the keyword and are visible in the current map view are shown. Results are not fixed to your home-pin area.
3
Clear (×) button resets the keyword and restores all listings
  1. While a keyword is typed in the search bar, look for a small × button that appears at the right end of the bar.
  2. Click the × button.
  3. The search bar should clear and all listings near your location should reappear in the panel.
✅ Expected: × clears the search bar. All nearby listings are shown again without any keyword filter applied.
B
Explore Area Search Mode
Pan the map freely to search any area — no saved pin required
4 checks
⚠ Make sure you are on the Listings tab. You need to be signed in.
4
Search Mode toggle appears in the Browse sidebar with "My Locations" selected by default
  1. Make sure you are on the Listings (Browse) tab.
  2. Look in the left sidebar, between the category filter pills and the radius buttons (3km / 5km / etc.).
  3. You should see two buttons side-by-side: "📍 My Locations" and "🗺 Explore Area".
  4. "My Locations" should be the highlighted/selected one by default.
✅ Expected: The Search Mode toggle is visible in the Browse sidebar. "📍 My Locations" is the active button. The toggle does NOT appear on the Saved or My Ads tabs.
5
Switching to Explore Area places a blue dot on the map and changes the circle to blue
  1. Click the "🗺 Explore Area" button in the sidebar.
  2. A notification/toast should briefly appear saying something like "🗺 Explore mode — pan the map to search any area".
  3. Look at the map: a small blue dot should appear at the centre of the map.
  4. The dashed radius circle should turn blue (it was orange before in My Locations mode).
  5. The label above the radius buttons should now read "Search Radius" instead of "Proximity Radius".
✅ Expected: Blue dot appears at map centre, dashed circle turns blue, toast notification shown, radius label updates to "Search Radius".
6
Panning the map in Explore mode reloads listings from the new area automatically
  1. While in Explore Area mode (from test 5), drag the map to a different part of Bangalore.
  2. While panning: watch the blue dot — it should move with the map centre in real-time. The blue circle should follow it.
  3. Stop panning and wait about 1 second.
  4. The listing panel should automatically reload and show listings from the new map centre area. You do not need to press any button.
✅ Expected: Blue dot tracks the map centre as you pan. About 1 second after stopping, listings in the panel update to show results from the new location.
7
Explore Area toggle is greyed out on Saved and My Ads tabs; returns to active on Listings tab
  1. While on the Listings tab (in either mode), click the ❤ Saved tab.
  2. Look at the Search Mode toggle area — the two buttons should appear faded/greyed out and cannot be clicked.
  3. Click the 📋 My Ads tab — same behaviour: toggle is greyed out.
  4. Click back to the Listings tab — the toggle should be fully active and clickable again.
✅ Expected: Search Mode toggle is visually dimmed on Saved and My Ads tabs (cannot switch modes). Returning to Listings tab restores the toggle as fully interactive.

Map Fly-To Behaviour
When you open a listing, the map should smoothly animate to show its pin
3 checks
8
Opening a listing in Browse mode flies the map to that listing's pin
  1. On the Listings (browse) tab, scroll the left panel to find a listing that is not near the centre of the current map view.
  2. Click that listing in the panel.
  3. The map should smoothly animate (fly) to show the listing's orange pin in view.
✅ Expected: Map smoothly flies to show the selected listing's pin. The listing detail opens in the panel.
9
Opening your own listing in My Ads flies the map in close (zoom 16)
  1. Open the 📋 My Ads tab from the tab bar.
  2. If you have any listings, click one.
  3. The map should fly very close to that listing's location — street-level zoom where you can clearly see the street name.
✅ Expected: Map zooms in very close (you can read individual street labels) to the listing's location.
10
Opening a saved listing flies the map in close and stays on the Saved tab
  1. Open the ❤ Saved tab.
  2. If you have saved any listings, click one from the panel.
  3. The map should fly in close to that listing's pin.
  4. The panel should stay on the Saved tab — it should NOT jump to the Listings tab.
✅ Expected: Map flies close to the saved listing's pin. You stay on the Saved tab — no tab switch happens.
